# Portfolio Style

This project is a writing-first portfolio for deep technical essays and opinion pieces about computation: GPUs, shaders, terminals, terminal emulation, software tools, and the future of software building.

The site should not feel like a marketing page, startup landing page, or generic personal portfolio. It should feel like an engineered reading object: precise, quiet, mechanical, and intentional.

## Core idea

- The page is a physical sheet.
- Writing is the product. UI exists to support reading, orientation, and structure.
- The portfolio focusing on adding micro-interactions.

## Visual language

- Draw from engineering drawings, architecture sheets, drafting tools, early computing manuals, and printed technical matter.
- Use hairline strokes, measured spacing, structural labels, and restrained framing.
- Keep the interface minimal and serious. Avoid decorative effects that do not help reading.
- The design should feel built, not branded.

## Typography

- Helvetica family for sheet writing and display titles.
- IBM Plex Mono for labels, code, metadata, and interface chrome.
- Archivo Black for the logo.
- Body text should read like a book or technical paper: calm, dense, and readable.

## Color

- Monochrome base with one controlled accent only.
- Accent color is Prussian Blue `#003153`.
- Use the accent only for meaningful signals such as section numbers, active states, link hover, markers, and diagram/data emphasis.
- Do not introduce decorative color.

## Current prototype

- Single centered white sheet on a white desk/background.
- The sheet is separated mainly by shadow, corner brackets, and paper presence.
- Layout is article-first and single-column.
- Sidenotes currently resolve into a Notes section below the article.
- Labels, dimension lines, edge marks, frames, and rulers should feel like document instruments, not app widgets.

## Motion

- Motion should be subtle, structural, and mechanical.
- Use motion for reveal, orientation, and instrument behavior.
- Avoid playful, bouncy, or decorative animation.
- If an interaction moves, it should feel like part of the paper system.

## Ruler principle

- The reading ruler is a fixed instrument attached to the sheet edge.
- Its behavior should communicate reading progress clearly.
- The ruler should not feel like browser chrome or a generic progress bar.

## Avoid

- Marketing-style hero layouts.
- Card-heavy UI.
- Decorative gradients, blobs, or ornamental textures.
- Large filled controls, heavy shadows, or soft SaaS styling.
- Trend-driven visual tricks that compete with the writing.
